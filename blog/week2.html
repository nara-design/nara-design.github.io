problem statement

News platforms focus too much on negative news stories without a balance of positive news stories. This oversaturation of negativity encourages doomscrolling; consequently, many news consumers leave in a worse mood than when they started.

so what?

To help me figure out a solution, I reframed the problem as a "how might we" question: how might we create a news platform that allows users to stay informed without being overwhelmed with negativity?

My answer to this question is to create web-based news platform that allows users to choose their preferred ratio of negative to positive news. This, on top of preventing users from seeing an endless sea of negative news headlines, also allows users some level of control over their news feed, thus enabling a degree of personalisation.

I also have the tentative idea of incorporating positive reminders within news articles to avoid users actively falling down the doomscrolling wormhole. However, as this isn't necessary for an MVP, I will have to see how I go.

field review

An analysis was done of existing news platforms with a focus on web-based platforms. I made note of what each platform does well and lacks in.

ai news

https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/

Does Well

  • clear purpose
  • consistent colour palette
  • back to top button
  • shareable to social media platforms

Could be improved

  • information overload (cluttered content, many columns, very little empty space)
  • a lot of ads (distracting from main content)
  • site name/logo missing from fixed header
  • unintuitive nav layout (the convention for top left corner to link back to the home page, but this site has facebook, twitter and linkedin icons instead; those could be in the footer instead)
  • main page continues scrolling for far too long
  • messy layout

artifact

https://artifact.news/

Does Well

  • presents news stories from a wide variety of sources
  • personalised "for you" page
  • sleek, comfortable ui design
  • shows how many reads each article has
  • save stats on what the user tends to prefer
  • allows user to save preferred news topics which can be edited at any time
  • very visually neutral, allows content to stick out

Could be Improved

  • lacks a filter system (after searching for something, only lets you endlessly scroll to find the thing you're looking for; there's no way to narrow down search results)
  • only available on mobile
  • requires creating an account to see news content

RNZ

https://www.rnz.co.nz/

Does Well

  • follows design conventions
  • simple navigation
  • variety of ways of presenting news (video, article, audio)
  • consistent, simple colour palette
  • adjusts with screen size

Could be Improved

  • navigation isn't universal (scrolls up with content, lacks "back to top" button)
  • scrolls for a looong time

the optimist daily

https://www.optimistdaily.com/

Does Well

  • simple layout, balance of content and empty space
  • easy to navigate
  • has search + filter capabilities
  • consistent, simple colour palette

Could be Improved

  • navigation isn't universal (scrolls up with the rest of the content and lacks "back to top" button to take the user up quickly)
  • purpose of the website isn't clearly stated from the beginning (only becomes clear after the user scrolls all the way to the bottom)

NZ herald

https://www.nzherald.co.nz/

Does Well

  • main content block is narrower
  • adjusts with screen size
  • lots of empty space (at least on a desktop screen)
  • variety of news mediums; text, audio, video
  • no ads
  • has back to top button

Could be Improved

  • navigation isn't universal (scrolls up with content - on all screen sizes)
  • back to top button only appears when you start scrolling up, isn't very obvious
  • scrolls for a long time
  • overload of categories to choose from in navigation
  • inconsistent colour palette (caused by overload of colour-coded categories)

thoughts

  • all news sites inevitably have some form of bias or another, so I like Artifact's approach of sourcing news from all sorts of sources
  • the easiest sites to navigate tend to be the ones with simple colour schemes
  • the navigation must be fixed in place, otherwise the user is given too much work to continue using the website
  • avoid too many columns (max of three on a desktop screen) to avoid clutter and overwhelming the user
  • make sure site adjusts to be accessible on both desktop and mobile (and in-between sizes)

branding

As the problem I chose to focus on is doomscrolling, I decided to take the "doom" and flip it upside-down, literally. And so the news platform is named The Daily Woopscroll.

stylescapes

I tried to create three distinctly different directions in these stylescapes to give myself a range of options. Each stylescape has the same core elements and the same focus on countering negativity.

The first stylescape has ripped paper textures to create a subtle link to newspaper, as well as a toned-down colour palette with the intention of conveying calmness. However, next to the other two stylescapes this option ends up looking dull instead of uplifting.

Though I thought the second stylescape is the strongest in terms of good vibes, I think the bright colours would be too distracting for a news platform. By comparison, the third option is ideal due to its shear simplicity. The minimalist styling is neutral enough for a news platform and the singular use of yellow gives it a bright, positive feeling. For these reasons I have decided to go with the third option for the branding of this project.
