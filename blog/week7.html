pecha kucha

reflective statement

Description: What happened?

I presented the work I'd done on this project so far on 12 slides at 20 seconds each.

Feeling: How did I feel?

I wasn't particularly nervous up to or during the presentation, surprisingly. However, I did get a bit flustered right at the beginning.

Evaluation: What went well, what didn't?

When my turn came up my mic suddenly stopped working so I had to quickly switch mics. That threw me off a bit, and I fumbled over what to say during the first two slides.

However, as soon as those two slides had passed, the rest of the presentation was fine.

Analysis: Why did/didn't things go well?

The mic probably stopped working because it's too old and unreliable. Fumbling at the start happened because of that and because I hadn't planned what to say during those two slides. But the rest of the presentation was fine because I'd gotten some practice in the previous week and so had a decent feel for the content/timing.

Conclusion: What did I learn?

I learned that I should plan more for presentations.

Action: What will I do differently next time?

Next time I will plan what to say for every single slide, not just some of them. I will also probably get a new more reliable mic, and test it thoroughly beforehand.

user testing feedback

  • too much spacing with the nav links; try a button style with more distinction between each link (rule of three)
  • feels off that header colours blend into content colours

  • log out confirmation background feels visually strange and inconsistent with rest of design

next steps

  • adjust header sizing/colours/spacing/etc
  • make header more distinguishable from content—underline or something
  • adjust mobile header to match desktop header
  • separate sign up/login pages
  • change logout confirmation background (solid colour instead)
  • adjust colour proportions
  • replace placeholder images with actual images
