Hiwa-i-te-rangi

Ko te whetū whakamutunga ko Hiwa-i-te-rangi, he hononga ki te whakapau kētanga o te tau whakapai. Ko te whetū whakapau tūmanako hoki tēnei. I te tikanga Māori, ka whakamahi ngā tāngata i tēnei whetū hei whakatakoto i ō rātou hiahia me ō rātou moemoeā mō te tau, kua mōhiotia ināianei hei whakapau whakaritenga Tau Hou. Ka taea e koe te titiro ki tēnei whetū hei whakatakoto whāinga me ngā whakapau whakaritenga mō te tau e whai mai ana.

The final star is hiwa-i-te-rangi which is connected to the promise of a prosperous season. It is also known as the wishing star. Māori traditionally would use this star to set their desires and dreams for the year, commonly known now as New Year’s resolutions. You can look upon this star to set goals and resolutions for the year to come.
