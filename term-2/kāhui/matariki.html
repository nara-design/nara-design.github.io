Torotoro nga whetu o Matariki

Explore the stars of Matariki

Mānawa maiea te putanga o Matariki
Mānawa maiea te ariki o te rangi
Mānawa maiea te mātahi o te tau

Hail the rise of Matariki
Hail the lord of the sky
Hail the New Year

Matariki, he whetū whakatipuranga e mōhiotia ana i te Pleiades, he whetū whakapau kētanga ā Taurus. Mō te nuinga o ngā ahurea, he whakapau kētanga ā Matariki he whakapau kētanga ki ngā whakanui, ki te whakatipu whakaruruhau, ki te āhuarangi, me te ora hoki. I roto i te ahurea Māori, ka whakaritea te whakapau kētanga o Matariki hei tohu i te Tau Hou Māori, he mōhio ko te Mātahi o te Tau. Ina whānau mai a Matariki i te rangi o te ata, ka karanga whakarauika i te tangata kia whakamihi i te hunga wheturangatira, kia whakanui i te wā o nāianei, kia whakatakoto whakamua i te wā e whai ake ana. He wā hei whakakore i te whakapau kētanga ki te whakatomo i te whānau me ngā hoa - hei whakanui i te kai, i te waiata, i te tākaro me te haka.

I te tau 2022, ka whiwhi te Matariki hei rā whakanui tūmatanui i Aotearoa, i te rā 14 o Hōngongoi. Kua whiwhi te Te Ture mō te Hararei Tūmatanui o te Kāhui o Matariki 2022, he whakaae, he whakanui hoki i te ahurea Māori, i te marama o Ēpure. Nā te mea, ko te Maramataka Māori, koia nei e whakarite ana i ngā rā, kāore i te ēperehia i te Maramataka Īngarangi, ka rerekē ēnei rā i ia tau. I te tau 2023, ka whakanuia te Matariki i te 11 o Hōngongoi ki te 17 o Hōngongoi, ā, ka whakanui ai i te rā tūmatanui ā-motu i te 14 o Hōngongoi. He whakairinga whaiaro ki te Maramataka Māori tēnei, he mea whakanui i Aotearoa te mātauranga Māori.

Matariki, also known as Pleiades, is a cluster of stars that is part of the Taurus constellation. For many cultures, Matariki holds significance in celebrations, agriculture, weather, and life. In Māori culture, the rising of Matariki marks the Māori New Year, known as te Mātahi o te Tau. When Matariki appears in the morning sky, it is a call for people to come together, honor the departed, celebrate the present, and plan for the future. It’s a time to spend with whānau and friends – to enjoy kai (food), waiata (song), tākaro (games) and haka.

In 2022, Matariki became an official public holiday in Aotearoa New Zealand. The Te Kāhui o Matariki Public Holiday Act 2022, which acknowledges and celebrates Māori culture, came into effect in April 2022. Because Māori follow the Māori lunar calendar, not the European calendar, the dates for Matariki change every year. In 2023, we celebrate Matariki from 11 July to 17 July, with a national public holiday on 14 July. It is an important addition to the calendar as Aotearoa celebrates mātauranga Māori.
